Saving

  1. Eliminate Debt

    2. Prioritize repayments, pay small debts first, then focus on larger debts. Cut down uncessecary expenses, and increase income if possible. Stay disciplined to build a strong foundation for your future.

  2. Reduce Expenses

    3. Track spending, re-negotiate your bills, cook at home, use public transportation, and consider sharing expenses.

  3. Record Expenses

    4. Use as a simple piece of paper, a budgeting app, or a notepad. Make it a habit to track every expense daily, categorize them, and review regularly so you won't forget.

  4. Set Saving Goals Based on your Salary

    5. Calculate your monthly income and fixed/essential expenses. Determine a realistic portion of your salary to save each month, a minimum of 20% if possible. Set goals for your savings, considering short-term and long-term objectives. Adjust your goals based on your salary and financial situation as times passes.

Investing

  1. Set Investment Goals

    2. Consider your financial objectives, how old you are, how tolerant to risk you are, and your current financial situation. Determine whether you're investing for short-term goals, like buying a car or long-term goals, like retirement. Set specific goals, for example, saving a certain amount of money by a certain date or achieving a target portfolio value.

  2. Choose an Investing Account

    3. Consider factors such as fees, investment options, account minimums, etc. Determine whether you want a traditional brokerage account, a retirement account like an IRA or 401k, or a specialized account like a college savings account.

  3. Invest 15% of your Salary

    4. Choose diversified investment options such as index funds or ETFs that align with your financial goals. Regularly review your investments and adjust as your salary and financial situation changes.

  4. Build up your portfolio

    5. First of all, it's important to start by defining your investment objectives and your age. After that, you can then buy assets, depending on what you prefer, such as: stocks, bonds, and real estate. Keeping an eye on your investments allows for adjustments as your salary changes, causing your portfolio to stay aligned with your goals.

  5. Reminder: Be Tolerant to Risk

    6. Start by educating yourself about different investment options, don't invest in what you don't understand . Gradually expose yourself to riskier investments by starting with small amounts and gradually increasing your exposure over time as you become more comfortable, this will cause you to worry less, since you know how things work. Maintain a long-term mindset, avoid making impulsive decisions based on short-term market changes.

Budgeting

  1. Organize Important Expenses

    2. Start by creating categories for your expenses, such as housing, utilities, groceries, transportation, debt repayments, etc. Prioritize fixed/essential expenses like housing, utilities, and debt repayments, ensuring they're covered first. Set aside savings for emergencies in you bank account.

  2. Budget Based on Importance Hierachy

    3. Essential expenses such as housing, utilities, groceries, and debt repayments should be your first priority. Unnecessary expenses, like dining out, entertainment, and jewelery, should be budgeted for last and adjusted based on available funds after covering essentials.

  3. Record Net Income

    4. Start by calculating your total income from all sources, including wages, bonuses, and other earnings. Deduct taxes, retirement contributions, etc.

  4. Review Budget Based on Profit/Deficit

    5. Analyze each expense to identify areas where you can cut back or reallocate funds to eliminate the deficit and achieve financial balance.
